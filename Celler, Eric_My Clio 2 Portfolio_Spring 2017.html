Essay 1: Exploratory Data Analysis

Exploratory Data Visualization Essay

My first visualization essay explores the data in the New Nation Votes Dataset. Within this dataset, we're able to look at early election results in America. By subsetting the data to only include the state of Georgia, I was able to analyze election returns in the state of Georgia from the period 1789-1824. The findings within the data indicate that during this period the Jeffersonian Party saw a rise in popularity in the state of Georgia. Essay 2: Mapping

Mapping Visualization Essay

This essay attempts to take a broad view examination of population changes in the state of Georgia before and after the Civil War. Using the tools of mapping in R combined with the history data library, I was able to look at the populations of cities for which there was data available. By looking at these factors, I was able to see that, prior to the Civil War, Savannah by far had the highest population. However, following the Civil War and Atlanta saw itself boom in population thanks to the reconstruction efforts and its superior rail network. For more analysis, please see the essay linked above.

Essay 3: Text Analysis

Text Analysis Visualization Essay

By taking a text analysis approach to the WPA Former Slave Narratives dataset, this essay attempts to look at the language used in the narratives of former slaves to get a sense of what the lives of former slaves were like. By looking at the language used in the narratives, and, in this essay specifically, narratives of slaves in Georgia, one is able to get a sense of the cultural, economic, and social factors impacting slaves' lives. Ultimately, it appears that the text reveals a trend of a concern for family, religion, and freedom; things we would expect to see emerge from the lives of any human, and especially humans living under the conditions of slavery. For more analysis, please see the essay linked above.
